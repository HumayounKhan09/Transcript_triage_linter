Agent: Second, there is no such thing as a "lender credit opportunity" that reduces your interest rate without a formal refinance. Agent: Loan modifications do exist, but they're available only in specific circumstances — typically when a borrower is experiencing a documented financial hardship that makes the current payment unaffordable. Caller: I'm not in hardship. My loan is current.

Agent: It's a significant red flag. Federal law — specifically the Mortgage Assistance Relief Services rule, or MARS Rule, enforced by the FTC — prohibits companies from collecting upfront fees before providing mortgage assistance services. A legitimate broker would not ask for payment before delivering results.
Caller: He said the five hundred dollars was a processing fee, not payment for results.
Agent: That framing is a common workaround attempt. The MARS Rule is designed to prevent exactly that structure — taking money upfront for a claimed mortgage-related service. You should not pay this person anything.
