Fannie typically works with mortgage bankers and Freddie more with savings institutions, but in practice they operate similarly for borrowers.
Caller: And a portfolio loan?
Agent: A portfolio loan is one that the originating lender kept on its own books — didn't sell to anyone. Banks, credit unions, and some specialty lenders do this. Portfolio loans sometimes have more flexible underwriting, but the terms and servicing can vary more widely.
