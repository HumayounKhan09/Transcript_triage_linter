Caller: And the other kind?
Agent: The other is called an all-in or all-inclusive policy. That one extends into the unit and covers fixtures and sometimes even improvements. Some policies fall somewhere in between — they cover original fixtures but not upgrades you made.
Caller: So how do I know which one my HOA has?
Agent: You'd want to request a copy of the HOA's master policy declarations page. Sometimes it's in the condo documents you received at closing. The key thing to look for is called the coverage type or scope of coverage. If it says "bare walls" or "single entity," that means less interior coverage. If it says "all-in," you have more coverage from the HOA side.
Caller: Okay. So where does the HO6 come in?
Agent: The HO6 is a condo owner's policy that you purchase individually. It fills the gaps. It covers your personal property — your furniture, electronics, clothing.
